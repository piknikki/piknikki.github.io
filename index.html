Hey, what the heck is a Scrum Master, anyway??

I'm so glad you asked! Scrum is like a mix of executive coaching, project management, and administrative assistant. A Scrum Master is a leader, but in a supportive way -- the scrum doesn't lead by delegating, she leads by giving support to the team, and removing any obstacles. She knows when to listen (80%) and when to speak up (20%), and is a rabid protector of her team. Letting the team self-organize and find their path of least resistance is the only way to give them the autonomy they need so they can achieve the greatest success in the goals of the organization. This hands-off approach is like free-range parenting, but for leadership. Youâ€™re letting the team figure out what works best for them. Scrum is just one framwork for practicing Agile Methodology.


Scrum is typically used for software development teams, but can be used in any context (which is one of the things I love most about it), including education, the military, and businesses that aren't doing software. It has a solid foundation in emotional intelligence, because you need to be able to feel the feels, communicate in an effective way, and keep a positive and growth-oriented mindset. Empathy is essential. The Scrum Master empowers their team with autonomy and trust, which is what we all want! Turns out, when you give people that freedom, they can change the world. I love scrum because, IMHO, it's the best way to help a development team. And now that I'm studying software development, that's going to be increasingly important to my work!
